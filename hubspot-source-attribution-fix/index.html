The HubSpot Original Source Attribution Fix

First-touch source quietly gets overwritten, and it takes your attribution with it. Here is why it happens and the pattern I use to capture and protect it for good.

HubSpot's Original Source property is supposed to record how a contact first found you. In practice it drifts. An integration recreates the contact, an import runs without the right settings, or analytics re-stamps a returning visitor, and a lead who came in through organic search now reads as paid or direct. Because so much reporting leans on this one field, a single overwrite does not break one record, it silently distorts every channel and campaign roll-up that depends on it. This is one of the most common and most expensive attribution bugs in HubSpot, and it is fixable.

Why It Breaks

Original Source is written at contact creation, but it is not truly immutable. Re-creation by integrations, imports that touch source fields, offline contact merges, and analytics re-identification can all re-stamp it. The result is attribution that looks precise and is quietly wrong, and teams making budget decisions on top of numbers that rewrote themselves last quarter.

The Fix: Capture and Lock

The pattern is to stop trusting the native field as your source of truth. The moment a contact is created, I capture the genuine first-touch source and drill-down details into dedicated custom properties, then lock those properties so nothing downstream can overwrite them. All attribution reporting reads from the protected properties, not the field that keeps moving.

Guardrails in Workflow

Locking is enforced, not hoped for. Using workflows and custom-coded actions with write-once logic, the protected source is set a single time and then defended against every later trigger that would otherwise change it. The same guardrail approach catches edge cases like merges and re-imports so the fix does not erode over the months after I hand it over.

Recovering What You Can

Where original values were ever captured into a backup property, an export, or are reconstructable from form-submission and page-view history, I rebuild a clean first-touch source. Where the history is genuinely gone, I will tell you so rather than invent it, and we protect everything from that point forward so the data only gets more trustworthy from here.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does HubSpot Original Source change on its own?

Original Source is set when a contact is first created, but several things can re-trigger or overwrite source attribution later: an integration recreating contacts, an import without the right settings, or analytics re-stamping a known visitor. When that happens, a contact who first found you through organic search can suddenly look like they came from a paid campaign or a direct visit, and months of attribution quietly rewrites itself.

Can you recover source data that was already overwritten?

Sometimes. If the original values were ever captured into a backup property, an export, or are reconstructable from form submission and page-view history, we can often rebuild a clean first-touch source. Where the history is truly gone, we cannot invent it, but we can stop the bleeding immediately and protect everything from that point forward.

How do you stop it from happening again?

I capture the true first-touch source into a dedicated, locked property the moment a contact is created, then use that protected property as the source of truth for all attribution reporting instead of the native field that keeps shifting. Workflow guardrails make sure it is written once and never silently overwritten.
