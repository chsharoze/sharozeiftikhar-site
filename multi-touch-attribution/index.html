Multi-Touch Attribution in HubSpot

First-touch, linear, and full-path attribution built on a data model that holds up, so the credit your reports assign matches how revenue actually happened.

Most attribution arguments are really data-model arguments in disguise. When marketing and sales disagree about what drove a deal, the root cause is usually that the CRM cannot answer the question cleanly: touches are missing, associations are broken, or the original source got overwritten the first time someone re-submitted a form. I build attribution in HubSpot from the plumbing up, so the model runs on data that is actually trustworthy, and the report becomes something leadership stops second-guessing.

First-Touch and Last-Touch

First-touch tells you what creates demand. Last-touch tells you what closes it. Both are single-model views, and both are useful as long as you know which question each one answers. I make sure the original source and the closing touch are captured correctly and protected from being overwritten, so these views stay honest over the life of a contact.

Linear and Full-Path Models

For longer, multi-stakeholder B2B journeys, single-touch models hide most of the story. Linear attribution spreads credit evenly across every touch, while full-path and W-shaped models weight the moments that matter most: first touch, lead conversion, and deal creation. I implement these so a campaign that quietly assists dozens of deals stops looking like it did nothing.

The Data Model Underneath

Attribution is only as good as the associations it reads. I design contact-to-deal associations, lifecycle syncing, and offline-touch capture so that the journey HubSpot records is the journey that actually happened. This is the unglamorous work that decides whether any model on top of it is meaningful, and it is where I spend most of the effort.

Reporting Leadership Trusts

The deliverable is not a model, it is a decision people are willing to act on. I build dashboards that show more than one attribution view side by side, label what each one measures, and make the assumptions explicit, so the conversation moves from arguing about the numbers to deciding what to do with them.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which attribution model should we use?

It depends on the question you are answering. First-touch tells you what creates demand, last-touch tells you what closes it, and linear or full-path models spread credit across the whole journey. Most teams need more than one, reported side by side, rather than a single model treated as the truth. I help you pick the models that match how your revenue actually happens.

Why does HubSpot attribution show numbers we do not trust?

Almost always it is the data model underneath, not the report. Broken contact-to-deal associations, inconsistent lifecycle syncing, untracked offline touches, and original-source values that get overwritten all corrupt attribution before the model runs. Fixing attribution starts with fixing the plumbing the model reads from.

Do we need Marketing Hub Enterprise for this?

HubSpot's native multi-touch revenue attribution reporting requires Marketing Hub Enterprise, but you do not need it to get meaningful attribution. With a clean data model and the right properties, custom-coded workflow actions, and reporting, you can build first-touch and path-based views on lower tiers. I scope to what your portal supports.
