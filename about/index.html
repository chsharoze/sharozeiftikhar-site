About Sharoze Iftikhar

Senior RevOps Consultant, AI Solutions Architect, and HubSpot Certified Trainer.

I design HubSpot revenue systems and build AI automation for B2B revenue teams. My work sits where two things meet: the operational plumbing that makes a CRM actually drive revenue, and the AI tooling that takes the repetitive, judgement-heavy work off a team's plate. The throughline is the same in both cases - make the system do the work reliably, so the people don't have to.

On the HubSpot side, I'm a HubSpot Certified Trainer with deep platform expertise. I've spent years inside the parts of HubSpot most consultants never touch: workflow actions and their real-world guardrails, CRM architecture and association schema, calculated properties, and custom coded actions that extend the platform beyond what the native toolset allows. I know not just what the UI exposes, but what the public API actually supports - and where the gaps are that quietly break revenue processes.

The proof is in what I've shipped. I built the Business Days Calculator, a HubSpot marketplace app published on the HubSpot App Marketplace that brings real business-day logic - holidays, working calendars, SLA timers - into HubSpot workflows. Alongside client work, I run revopsdev.com, my RevOps consulting practice, and networkdays.io, a SaaS product. Building and operating my own products keeps me honest: I live with the same tradeoffs I recommend to clients.

My approach is minimal and deliberate. I'd rather ship one thing that works than five things that demo well and break in production. Every tool, integration, and dependency has to earn its place. When the answer is to do less, I do less. That discipline carries into how I build AI systems too - for knowledge retrieval I favour hybrid CAG over naive RAG, because grounding an agent in structured, queryable truth beats stuffing it with fuzzy chunks and hoping the embeddings line up. Correct beats clever, every time.

I work with clients worldwide - across time zones, mostly async, always with a bias toward systems that keep running whether or not I'm in the room. If you're a revenue team trying to make HubSpot do more, or you want AI automation that's actually wired into how your business operates rather than bolted on, that's the work I do.

From policy research to RevOps architecture

Before the RevOps and AI work, I was a researcher. Around 2019 I published policy and security analysis as a Research Fellow at SASSI University in Islamabad, and on Strafasia, writing on nuclear deterrence and strategic stability in South Asia. Those pieces ran under the bylines M. Sharoze Iftikhar and Muhammad Sharoze Iftikhar: same person, same chsharoze handle I still use on GitHub, X, and email. You can read that work on my Strafasia author archive, including pieces like India's Delusional Nuclear Strategy.

It is not as much of a leap as it looks. My training is in Public Administration, at NUST's School of Social Sciences and Humanities, and policy research is fundamentally about modelling complex systems, reasoning under incomplete information, and being precise about what a piece of evidence does and does not support. That is the same discipline I now apply to revenue systems: map how the thing actually works, design for the real constraints, and refuse to claim more than the data shows. The subject changed from deterrence to pipelines. The way of thinking did not.
