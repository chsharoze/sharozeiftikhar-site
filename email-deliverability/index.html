Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between SPF, DKIM, and DMARC?

SPF lists which servers are allowed to send for your domain. DKIM cryptographically signs your mail so receivers can verify it was not tampered with. DMARC ties the two together, tells receivers what to do when a message fails, and sends you reports. You need all three aligned for reliable inbox placement, and DMARC is what turns the other two from optional into enforced.

Why are our HubSpot emails landing in spam?

Usually a mix of authentication gaps and reputation damage. Missing or misaligned DMARC, an unverified sending domain, sending to stale or purchased lists, and high bounce or complaint rates all push you toward the spam folder. The fix is to authenticate properly, clean the list, and rebuild reputation by sending to engaged contacts first.

Does list hygiene really affect deliverability?

Yes, more than most teams expect. Mailbox providers watch how recipients react to your mail. Sending to dead addresses, spam traps, and unengaged contacts raises bounces and complaints, which lowers your sender reputation and hurts delivery for everyone on the list. Suppressing the wrong contacts is one of the highest-leverage deliverability fixes there is.
